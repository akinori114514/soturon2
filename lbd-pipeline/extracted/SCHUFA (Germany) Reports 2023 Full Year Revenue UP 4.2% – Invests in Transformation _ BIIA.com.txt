                   SCHUFA was able to increase its sales even in the
         ×         recession year of 2023, despite declining consumer
                   spending among Germans. SCHUFA Holding AG’s sales
                   rose from EUR 267.3 million in the previous year to EUR
                   276.1 million (+ 3.3%). Earnings before taxes and
                   financial result (EBIT) rose by 5.3% year-on-year to EUR
                   78.1 million. The SCHUFA Group, including its
                   subsidiaries Forteil GmbH (bonify), ClariLab GmbH
                   and finAPI GmbH, also increased its sales by 4.2% to
                   EUR 283.8 million.

                   Increase revenue through creditworthiness,
                   identification, fraud prevention and compliance

                   The SCHUFA Group’s increase in sales extends across
                   all four business areas – creditworthiness,
                   identification, fraud prevention and compliance – but is
                   of varying degrees. Sales in the creditworthiness area
                   rose by 3.4% to EUR 240.1 million in 2023 compared to
                   2022. The identification (+ 4.3%) and fraud
                   prevention (+ 0.6%) business areas also increased
                   compared to the previous year (EUR 21.9 million to EUR
                   22.8 million and EUR 11.7 million to EUR 11.8 million
                   respectively). The compliance business area saw the
                   strongest percentage increase, almost doubling its
                   sales compared to the previous year (EUR 2.2 million to
                   EUR 4.2 million, + 87.1%). This is largely due to the
                   success of the KYC solutions with ClariLab.

                   Business with corporate customers grew more strongly
                   in 2023 than that with private customers. SCHUFA
                   generated sales of EUR 197.2 million with corporate
                   customers (+ 4.4% compared to 2022). The main driver
                   was a stable growth rate of SCHUFA credit products.
                   But increasing digitization and regulatory challenges in
                   the economy also led to significantly stronger growth
                   rates in the areas of identification, compliance and
                   fraud prevention. In the private customer area, which
                   includes SCHUFA’s subscription products as well as
                   tenant information, group sales rose from EUR 82.2
                   million to EUR 85.3 million (+ 3.7%). This can be
                   attributed to the growth in the rental market, which
                   was still recorded in 2023, as well as increased interest
                   in proving one’s own creditworthiness and protecting                                                                      
                   consumers against identity theft.

                   management value chain

                                               SCHUFA is more than just

                                               Tanja Birkholz, CEO of
                                               SCHUFA. “We were able to
                                               significantly increase our
                                               sales in 2023, especially in
                                               the area of ​compliance – the
                                               prevention of money
                   laundering and terrorist financing is a strategic focus of
                   the group. SCHUFA will expand its offering in order to
                   transform itself from a credit agency into a technology
                   provider in the area of ​regulation for its customers. We
                   are already active not only in the area of ​credit
                   information, but in the entire risk management value
                   chain.”

                   The Group’s EBIT fell by 8.7% year-on-year to EUR
                   63.4 million. The reason for this is the Group’s
                   investments as part of the transformation of
                   SCHUFA. This includes in particular the purchase of
                   the FinTech Forteil GmbH with its app bonify. To
                   implement its strategy for more explainability,
                   transparency and a stronger focus on consumers,
                   SCHUFA bought the Fintech at the end of 2022 and
                   thus enabled private individuals for the first time to
                   view their individual SCHUFA basic score digitally and
                   free of charge via bonify at any time. Since this year,
                   private individuals can also view negative SCHUFA
                   entries in the bonify app and be notified of initial
                   negative entries.

                   The annual profit of SCHUFA Holding AG was
                   impacted by a one-off special effect of EUR 12
                   million in the financial result. The reason was an
                   adjustment of the investment book value of finAPI
                   GmbH.
